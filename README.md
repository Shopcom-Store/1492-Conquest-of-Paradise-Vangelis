1492: Conquest of Paradise is the 1992 soundtrack album by Greek composer Vangelis, directed by Ridley Scott for the film 1492: Conquest of Paradise. It stands as one of Vangelis’s most celebrated works, blending his signature electronic orchestration with choral, ethnic, and Renaissance elements.

The album opens with the iconic title track, “Conquest of Paradise,” featuring a powerful, slow-building melody sung in pseudo-Latin by the English Chamber Choir. This piece became a massive international hit, symbolizing both the majesty and the tragic scope of Columbus’s voyage. Other key tracks include “Opening,” which establishes the film’s exploratory and spiritual themes, and “Monastery of La Rabida,” a meditative, flute-driven piece capturing Columbus’s determination.

Vangelis masterfully contrasts the vast, mysterious ocean (“Light and Shadow,” “Moxica and the Horse”) with the clash of Old and New Worlds, using electronic textures, sampled voices, acoustic guitars, and percussion. The album’s emotional depth—ranging from triumphant hope to somber reflection—transcends the film’s mixed reception. Despite criticism of historical inaccuracies in the movie, the score remains a standalone masterpiece, frequently used in trailers, sports events, and ceremonies (e.g., the 1992 Barcelona Olympics). It exemplifies Vangelis’s ability to evoke epic narratives and human struggle through purely instrumental music.
